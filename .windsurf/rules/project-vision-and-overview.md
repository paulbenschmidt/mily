The app is named "Mily" and is a social timeline app where users can record and reflect on life events then share them with others.

## Vision

### Mission
- Mily exists to help people understand themselves, and life, better by encouraging self-reflection through a visually engaging timeline of their life events and opportunities to reflect on them.
- The supplementary mission is to deepen relationships with others by allowing users to share their timelines with friends and family, renewing appreciation for how multi-faceted the lives of others are.

### Core Features
1. Timeline Creation: Users can record Major, Minor, and Other life events; add notes and photos to each event.
2. Timeline Sharing: Users can share their timelines with friends and family, and can set privacy levels for their timelines and individual events.

### Value Proposition
- By showing one's life visually, users can appreciate their depth and richness of experiences accumulated over their life lived.
- Users will experience emotions ranging from perspective ("Wow, it's been 8 years since I chose to change careers") to sonder ("You experienced so many different things before we met.")
### Foils/Anti-Themes
- Journaling lacks the visual component and doesn't visually display events spatially. Additionally, it's not as fun to share written words about past life events with someone else.
- Instagram "flattens" the events so that, for example, an everyday visit to a coffee shop occupies the same space as a major life event like getting married. Viewers don't have the ability to see what has salience/permanence/relevance over time.

### Target Audience
- The target audience will likely be users 35+ who have a wealth of past experiences to reflect on to:
    - reflect on them in order to live the second half of their life with intention
    - to share the experiences with loved ones in an easy-to-share format that doesn't overwhelm (helping younger generations appreciate the older generations)
- My hope is that this will encourage self-reflection and self-appreciation, along with deepening relationships.

### Customer Experience
- The app should have a clean, minimalist UI that doesn't draw attention to itself but instead focuses the user on the content within the app.
- The app should be easy to use and navigate.

### Behavioral Philosophy
- Users will likely engage with the platform in spurts: every few months to reflect on past experiences or when meeting new people or getting to know friends/family better. The platform will not encourage daily active users; it won't be "in their face".

### Themes and Elements
- The core theme of the app is lines. In cases where the user is self-reflecting, this line is clean, crisp, and minimal. In cases where the user is viewing other people's timelines, there is an element of lines intersecting and weaving.
- Color philosophy should be muted and neutral with selective pops of color for screens where the attention isn't the user's content.
- The typography system pairs Inter's humanist warmth for content and reflection with Geist's geometric precision for UI chrome and metadata.

### Privacy & Trust Philosophy
Mily exists to create a safe space for reflection and connection. That means your trust is everything.
Your data, your control:
- Your stories are yours—export or delete them anytime
- Your timeline content and reflections are private by default and will never be sold to advertisers or data brokers
- We may develop features that let you share your timeline with services you trust (like therapy apps or community platforms), but sharing is always opt-in and under your control

How we operate:
- Privacy features are free for everyone, always
- We use analytics to improve the app, but never mine your content for profit
- We may share anonymized, aggregated insights with researchers or partners whose work aligns with our mission
- We keep the lights on through optional paid features (storage, photos, themes), not by compromising your privacy

Our commitment: We're building Mily to be sustainable without being extractive. If we ever face a choice between growth and our mission to help people reflect and connect authentically, we choose the mission.
